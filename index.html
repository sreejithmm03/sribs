Executive Summary

During the reporting period (August 2024 – July 2026), I actively contributed to the academic, research and institutional development of KSCSTE–Srinivasa Ramanujan Institute for Basic Sciences through original research, conference presentations, invited lectures, outreach programmes, organisation of academic events, institutional documentation and digital initiatives. This report presents a concise overview of the research achievements, academic activities and institutional responsibilities undertaken during my tenure as a Postdoctoral Fellow.

Research Highlights

My research during the reporting period has focused primarily on Modular Forms, Siegel Modular Forms, Jacobi Forms, Half-Integral Weight Modular Forms, and related areas in analytic and algebraic number theory.

Submitted Manuscripts

Algebraicity of Certain Partial Sums involving Hecke Eigenvalues of Siegel Cusp Forms of Degree Two

Submitted Siegel Modular Forms

Generalizes the algebraicity results of Kohnen–Luca from elliptic modular forms to Siegel modular forms of genus two by studying partial sums of normalized Hecke eigenvalues.

In this work, we prove the algebraicity of certain partial sums involving the normalized Hecke eigenvalues of Siegel cusp forms of degree two. We establish that, beyond a finite range, these partial sums cannot all remain algebraic, thereby extending earlier results from the elliptic setting to genus two.

A Certain Self-Adjoint Operator on Jacobi Forms and Characterisations of Maass Space

Submitted Jacobi Forms

Introduces a self-adjoint operator on Jacobi forms and derives new characterisations of the Maass space through inner-product identities.

This work establishes an inner-product relation connecting Siegel newforms and Jacobi newforms. Using this relation, a natural self-adjoint operator on Jacobi forms is obtained, leading to new characterisations of Siegel newforms lying in the Maass space.

Manuscripts Under Preparation

Independence Between Eigenvalues of Siegel Modular Forms of Degree Two

Under Preparation Siegel Modular Forms

Studies multiplicity-one phenomena by investigating ratios of Hecke eigenvalues associated with genus two Siegel eigenforms.

This work generalizes a theorem of Choi–Lim from elliptic modular forms to genus two Siegel modular forms by proving that finiteness of eigenvalue ratios forces proportionality of the underlying eigenforms.

Theory of Newforms of Half-Integral Weight Modular Forms of Level 8N

Under Preparation Half-Integral Weight

Develops a newform theory for half-integral weight cusp forms of level 8N using trace relations and Shimura–Shintani correspondences.

The project develops structural results on newforms of half-integral weight, establishes plus-space properties, and investigates the Eichler–Zagier correspondence in the setting of higher levels.

Professional Growth During the Reporting Period

The reporting period at KSCSTE–Srinivasa Ramanujan Institute for Basic Sciences has been marked by sustained growth in research, academic outreach, institutional engagement, and professional development. Alongside pursuing independent research in modular forms and analytic number theory, I actively contributed to strengthening the institute through programme coordination, documentation, website development, and student mentoring.

The opportunity to present research at national conferences, deliver invited lectures, organise academic programmes, prepare institutional reports, and mentor undergraduate students significantly enhanced my skills in scientific communication, academic leadership, and institutional collaboration.

These diverse responsibilities have broadened my perspective as a researcher and educator while providing valuable experience in the functioning of a modern research institute. The experiences gained during this period have laid a strong foundation for future academic and institutional responsibilities.
